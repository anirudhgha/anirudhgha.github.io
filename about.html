03 : About me

Education

I did my Bachelors in Computer Engineering with Minors in Biology and English at Purdue University [2013-2017]. I then did my Masters in ECE again at Purdue [2017-2019]. I've continued on with the same research group to start my PhD, specialized in machine learning and Probabilistic Computing at Purdue University [2020-expected 2023].

Internships

I've completed 3 internships, of which the most recent one is most applicable to my experience in machine learning. I worked at Edgetensor Technologies, a startup out of Dallas, TX, for 6 months during the Covid lockdown. Here, I worked on improving accuracy for and reducing inference power and time for various machine learning models. Please see my works section for more details!

  • Ahmed Zeeshan Pervaiz, Brian M. Sutton, Lakshmi Anirudh Ghantasala, Kerem Y. Camsari, “Weighted p-bits for FPGA Implementation of Probabilistic Circuits” IEEE-TNNLS, 10.1109/TNNLS.2018.2874565, p.1-7, 2018
