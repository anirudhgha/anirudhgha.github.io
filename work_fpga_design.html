002 : FPGA Design for Sampling and NP-hard Problems

Over two years (2017-2019), I co-built two FPGA designs which would implement a boltzmann machine synchronously, and asynchronously, to expedite solving np-hard optimization problems like Maxcut and the Travelling Salesman Problem, as well as sampling problems. The probabilistic computer was built on Amazon Web Services (AWS), so that the general public could also access the machine to solve problems, with instructions to access found here.

This work has resulted in a 1st place award.

  1. 1st place Research Poster for Asynchronous FPGA modelling of p-bits (out of 18 total posters) [2018]

This work has resulted in 2 publications, one for each FPGA Design.

  1. A. Z. Pervaiz, B. M. Sutton, L. A. Ghantasala and K. Y. Camsari, "Weighted p-Bits for FPGA Implementation of Probabilistic Circuits," in IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, vol. 30, no. 6, pp. 1920-1926, June 2019, doi: 10.1109/TNNLS.2018.2874565.
  2. B. Sutton, R. Faria, L. A. Ghantasala, R. Jaiswal, K. Y. Camsari and S. Datta, "Autonomous Probabilistic Coprocessing With Petaflips per Second," in IEEE Access, vol. 8, pp. 157238-157252, 2020, doi: 10.1109/ACCESS.2020.3018682.
